Advanced Certification Program in Digital Manufacturing

Subsidized Certification Programme in Digital Manufacturing for professionals The objective of this program is to deliver an Advanced Certification Programme in Digital Manufacturing and Smart Factories to help build an ecosystem of tech savvy professionals. 300 Participants (100 per year) will learn through a comprehensive, six-month hybrid programme from a combination of academic and industry experts in Design, Manufacturing, Mechanical Engineering, Electronics Systems Engineering, Materials Engineering, Computer Science and Automation so as to provide an overview of all technologies under Industry 4.0.

  • Implementing Organization (PIO): CEFC of Smart Factory, Indian Institute of Science
  • Designated Officer of the PIO: Prof. Amaresh Chakrabarti
  • Industry Partner: Initial contribution by FSID and Contribution from the training participants
  • Budget: ₹ 5 Cr.

Mentoring and Supporting Startup Companies

Objective of this program is to support 100 start-ups in a three-year period on Smart Manufacturing and I4.0 and conduct three Hackathons. Benefits to participants: Affordability to attend the program will increase as the Govt provides 80% of the cost. As a result a wider community of people including those from forward thinking MSMEs can attend and gain the skills. 300 participants will be benefited by the hackathons from which 100 will be selected for subsequent mentoring and support. They will learn strategic and tactical tools that will help start-ups to become more systematic and efficient in problem identification, solution development and transition. Participants will also gain knowledge and skills on smart manufacturing and i4.0 technologies and leap forward for innovating technologies with which to develop affordable platform solutions for MSMEs to leapfrog into i4.0 ready organisations. This unique program by IISc in smart Manufacturing, i4.0 and related digital technologies will lead to better automation, predictive maintenance, self-optimization of process developments and, above all it enables the pathways to a new level of efficiency and responsiveness to customers not previously possible.

  • Implementing Organization (PIO): CEFC of Smart Factory, Indian Institute of Science
  • Designated Officer of the PIO: Prof. Amaresh Chakrabarti
  • Industry Partner: Tata Steel
  • Budget: ₹ 15.3 Cr.
