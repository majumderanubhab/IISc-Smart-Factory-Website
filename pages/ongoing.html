Smart Fastener Development

The project involves the development of smart fasteners at three distinct ranges to cater to various critical applications across segments. These smart fasteners will feature a reliable measuring technique, quantitative output, digital connectivity, instant monitoring with a device, part-level traceability, and customizable options to suit different segments. Engineered fastening is a $100 billion industry serving a wide range of sectors from automotive to infrastructure. A major challenge in this industry has been accurately measuring, predicting, or estimating the clamp loads applied to joints, which is essential for evaluating fastening reliability. With recent advancements and the miniaturization of sensor technology, there is renewed interest in developing smart fasteners. In critical heavy engineering applications, such smart fasteners could help predict maintenance schedules and provide greater assurance.

  • Implementing Organization (PIO): Department of Aerospace Engineering, IISc
  • Designated Officer of the PIO: Prof. D. Roy Mahapatra
  • Industry Partner: Indian Society of Assembly Technologies (I-SAT)
  • Budget: ₹ 3 Cr.

Machine for Friction & Linear Friction Welding of Long Pipes, rods and structural components

This project aims to enhance solid-state welding techniques such as friction stir welding and linear friction welding to enable the joining of longer components like pipes, rods, and parts of various shapes. These welding methods, which rely on friction-generated heat and pressure rather than melting, are known for producing high-quality, efficient welds, especially in materials like aluminium. However, current limitations in equipment design restrict the size of parts that can be effectively welded. The project addresses this challenge by developing a solution that allows for the welding of longer and more complex aluminium structures. This advancement will enable the fabrication of lightweight, all-aluminium assemblies suitable for automotive and other structural applications. It also opens the possibility of welding aluminium alloys that are typically difficult or impossible to join using conventional fusion welding methods, thereby improving manufacturing flexibility and overall weld integrity.

  • Implementing Organization (PIO): Department of Mechanical Engineering & Department of Design and Manufacturing, IISc
  • Designated Officer of the PIO: Prof. Satish V. Kailas
  • Industry Partner: ITW-India Pvt Ltd, Bengaluru
  • Budget: ₹ 3 Cr.

Material design and process for additive manufacturing of "cement-free" hollow-core members containing earth-based materials and recycled fines from construction and demolition waste

The project aims to establish a state-of-the-art advanced 3D printing and characterization facility focused on low-carbon manufacturing and research for prefabricated and modular building components. Key objectives include the development and optimization of print-head/nozzle designs and process parameters for printing hollow-core components using alkali-activated materials and construction and demolition (C&D) waste fines. A major focus is the creation of 3D printable earth-based alkali-activated materials by partially replacing fine M-sand (30-60%) with recycled fines and soil. The project will investigate the impact of these sustainable materials on the microstructure, mechanical performance, and durability of 3D printed structures. Further, it involves prototyping and on-site monitoring of printed hollow-core panels and wall components to demonstrate real-world applicability. This initiative aims to instil confidence among construction stakeholders and promote technology adoption, while enabling knowledge transfer and commercialization opportunities for Indian MSMEs in the sustainable construction sector.

  • Implementing Organization (PIO): Centre for Sustainable Technologies, IISc
  • Designated Officer of the PIO: Dr. Souradeep Gupta
  • Industry Partner: Tata Steel
  • Budget: ₹ 2.67 Cr.

Adaptable Mould and Formwork for 3D Volumetric Prefabricated Elements

The project focuses on designing and manufacturing adaptable mould and formwork equipment for producing Precast 3D volumetric construction units. This aims to replace costly imported machinery currently used in India, enhancing self-reliance in the capital goods sector. The solution targets increased scalability, cost-effectiveness, and production efficiency by enabling higher output per mould and greater flexibility in module design. It supports the growing demand for automation and prefabrication in the construction and infrastructure industries, offering a critical advancement for Indian capital goods in construction manufacturing.

  • Implementing Organization (PIO): Department of Design and Manufacturing, IISc
  • Designated Officer of the PIO: Prof. Vishal Singh
  • Industry Partner: hoMMission India Pvt. Ltd.
  • Budget: ₹ 2.5 Cr.

Common Data Environment for Situation Awareness in industry 4.0 applications in production environments

The project addresses critical gaps in existing Common Data Environments (CDEs), such as poor data integration, complex usability, limited analytical capabilities, and lack of seamless API interfacing. It aims to develop a digital CDE platform tailored for project-based workflows in Industry 4.0 settings. The platform will integrate diverse data sources and simplify user experience. Additionally, a Situation Awareness framework will be developed to enhance real-time monitoring, tracking, analysis, and reporting for stakeholders, improving overall project visibility and decision-making.

  • Implementing Organization (PIO): Department of Design and Manufacturing, IISc
  • Designated Officer of the PIO: Prof. Vishal Singh
  • Industry Partner: Desapex Engineering Consultants LLP, Bengaluru
  • Budget: ₹ 1.5 Cr.
