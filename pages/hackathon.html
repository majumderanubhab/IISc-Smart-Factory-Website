About the Hackathons

The MSME 4.0 TechSprint: National Hackathon Series 2025 is a flagship initiative supported by the Ministry of Heavy Industries (MHI), Government of India, and organised by Indian Institute of Science.

The series aims to identify and nurture innovative technology-based solutions that will help Indian Manufacturing Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) accelerate digital transformation and the adoption of Industry 4.0 technologies.

  • Each Hackathon will be an independent 2-day event bringing together innovators, researchers, start-ups, and MSME collaborators to develop actionable prototypes and business concepts that enhance the competitiveness of Indian manufacturing.
  • The most promising teams will be incubated at Indian Institute of Science under the MHI-supported MSME 4.0 Innovation Programme.


Hackathon Rules

# Criteria Name Description
1 Eligibility All participants must be aged over 18 years at the time of registration, and meet the eligibility criteria outlined below.
  • Educational Qualification: Applicants must hold at least a bachelor’s degree from a recognized university or institution. Open to professionals, researchers, and entrepreneurs.
  • Academic Performance: Candidates should demonstrate a strong academic record, typically reflected through above-average grades, academic achievements, or relevant project experience.
  • Relevant Skills: Applicants are expected to possess technical, social, managerial and/or entrepreneurial skills aligned with the themes and problem statements of the hackathon.
  • Documentation: All required documents — including degree certificates, academic transcripts, and supporting evidence of relevant skills or experience — must be submitted at the time of application.
2 Registration Each participant must register individually through the official portal. All required documents — including degree certificates, academic transcripts, and supporting evidence of relevant skills or experience — must be submitted at the time of application.
3 Team Formation Teams of 2–4 members will be formed during the hackathon through interaction sessions.
4 Problem Statement Selection Each team will initially select three problem statements from the published list. Organizers will evaluate and allocate one problem statement to each team. If multiple teams select the same problem, organizers may reassign teams and/or allocate a separate problem (which may not be from among the three problems proposed by the teams) to each team during the hackathon to ensure balanced participation, optimal fit, and diversity of solutions.
5 Mentorship & External Assistance Each team will be assigned one or more mentors for guidance based on expertise. Mentors will guide teams through idea validation, prototyping, and pitching. Mentors can advise on feasibility and approach but will not develop code or prototypes. External assistance beyond mentors is not allowed unless specifically approved by organizers.
6 Hackathon Structure The stages of the hackathon will be Revelation of Problem Statements, Methodological Instructions by faculty experts, Team formation, brainstorming, Mentor Allocation, Idea Pitch, Concept design, PoC prototype development and Final pitch. There will be multiple pitch rounds and Progress reviews in between. The detailed schedule will be shared with the registered participants.
7 Use of Resources Teams may use open-source libraries, frameworks, and publicly available APIs, respecting licensing terms. Pre-existing proprietary software, hardware, or code requires explicit prior approval. Most of the solution must be developed during the hackathon.
8 Deliverables Each team must submit the following:
  • Idea Pitch Deck (Round 1) – problem statement, solution approach, feasibility.
  • Prototype / MVP (Round 2) – working demo or functional PoC.
  • Final Presentation (Round 3) – prototype demo, business model, scalability plan.
  • Source Code & Documentation – to be shared with organizers for validation.
All deliverables must be in English and submitted through the official hackathon portal.
9 Code of Conduct Participants must act with integrity, professionalism, and respect.
  • Misconduct, harassment, discrimination, or offensive behaviour will lead to immediate disqualification.
  • Compliance with venue, safety, security, and IT policies is mandatory.
  • Alcohol, drugs, or prohibited substances are strictly banned.
10 Judging & Evaluation Judges will assess each criterion based on both qualitative and quantitative aspects, ensuring a balanced evaluation of creativity, technical feasibility, business potential, scalability, and teamwork. Evaluation will be conducted in three stages, aligned with the hackathon rounds:
  • Idea Pitch: Judges review clarity of problem understanding, originality of the solution, and potential business impact.
  • Prototype / PoC Development: Judges assess the working prototype for functionality, integration of Industry 4.0 technologies, feasibility, and demonstration of real-world applicability.
  • Final Pitch: Judges evaluate the complete solution, including technical performance, industrial scalability, sustainability, business model, novelty of the solution, and team presentation.
Judges’ decisions are final and binding. No appeals will be entertained. Tiebreakers may be decided by an additional Q&A round with shortlisted teams.
11 Intellectual Property (IP) & Incubation All teams participating in the hackathon should be committed to be involved, under IISc leadership, in subsequent technology development and incubation and adhere to the milestones and deliverables associated with these.
  • Selected teams will be committed to start the activities mentioned above from 1 Dec 2025.
  • Each incubate shall sign a legally binding MoU with FSID/IISc or its authorized partner, outlining obligations, deliverables, IP rights, confidentiality, and termination conditions.
  • Failure to execute work as planned in the project, show progress, or deliver as planned may lead to withdrawal of support, forfeiture of benefits, and recovery of funds or resources as per the MoU. Periodic performance reviews will determine continuation; non-compliance or breach will result in termination of incubation, and IISc’s decision under the MoU will be final and enforceable.
Intellectual Property (IP) created during the hackathon will remain with the team. If selected for incubation, IP terms (ownership, licensing, or revenue-sharing, if any) will be defined through a separate legal agreement with the incubator. By participating, teams grant organizers the right to use names, photos, solution descriptions, and demo videos for promotional purposes.
12 Confidentiality & Data Usage Any datasets provided by organizers must be used only for hackathon purposes. Teams must not disclose confidential data or proprietary information of the organizers, mentors, or partners. Violation may result in legal action.
13 Liability & Disclaimer Organizers are not responsible for:
  • Loss or damage of personal belongings.
  • Health issues or accidents not directly caused by organizational negligence.
  • Failure of internet, electricity, or external services.
  • Participants agree to indemnify organizers, sponsors, and partners against any claims arising from their participation.
14 General Provisions Organisers reserve the right to amend the schedule, rules, or evaluation process with prior notice. Misrepresentation of information, plagiarism, or rule violations will result in immediate disqualification. Participation in the hackathon implies acceptance of these rules and the organisers’ decisions as final and legally binding.
15 Awards & Recognitions No formal winners will be declared. Instead, a certain number of teams will be selected for incubation. Teams not selected for incubation will receive certificates of participation. Selected teams will be part of technology development and incubation projects at IISc, where each project will receive mentorship, funding support, prototyping support and connections with Industry partners.
